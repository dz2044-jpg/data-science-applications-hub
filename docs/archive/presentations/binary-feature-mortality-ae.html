The Core Purpose: A Visual Triage Engine

Binary Feature Mortality A/E turns a saved, mapped ruleset into a fast visual screen for underwriting and mortality signal review.

  • Config-backed ruleset review replaces spreadsheet scanning with an interactive visual triage surface.

  • Hit Rate on the x-axis shows prevalence, adjusted for when the feature became observable.

  • Mortality A/E on the y-axis shows actual-to-expected mortality performance without model smoothing.

  • The priority zone is the low-hit, high-A/E corner: hidden risks that deserve follow-up.

Slide 02

The Analytical Edge: Illuminating Machine Learning Blindspots

The module does not replace tree-based models. It complements them by surfacing univariate risk signals that global optimization can bury.

  • Claims are a tiny positive class in a very large policy population.

  • Tree-based models reduce global error by favoring higher-frequency features.

  • A rare rule can matter and still vanish if its hit rate is only around 0.5%.

  • This A/E workflow restores the pure univariate signal so rare but fatal indicators stay visible.

The Next Move: A Purpose-Specific Triage Copilot

AI belongs here first because the task is narrow, repetitive, and decision-oriented: review many rules quickly and decide what deserves action, monitoring, or a clearer summary.

  • This is the right first AI use case because the judgment is repetitive, the benefit is obvious, and the risk of over-interpretation is real.

  • Version one is guided actions, not open chat: Prioritize top rules, Explain selected rule, and Draft follow-up summary.

  • The backend flow stays disciplined: evidence packet to action-specific instruction to structured output to app-ready UI cards.

  • The guardrails matter as much as the model: prefer caution over drama, separate action from monitoring, and do not overclaim thin evidence.
